# Case Study 03: Corporate Office Relocation

## Project Overview and Background

**Company:** GlobalFinance Corp  
**Industry:** Financial Services  
**Project Name:** New York City Headquarters Relocation  
**Project Duration:** 18 months  
**Budget:** $42,500,000  
**Project Start Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Move Date:** June 28-30, 2024 (Weekend move)  
**Project End Date:** June 30, 2025 (includes 12-month stabilization)

### Background
GlobalFinance Corp is a multinational financial services firm with $125 billion in assets under management and 3,500 employees globally. The company's New York City headquarters currently houses 1,200 employees across 180,000 square feet in a 30-year-old building in Midtown Manhattan. The current lease expires September 30, 2024, and the building infrastructure no longer meets the company's technology, security, or workplace culture needs.

After a comprehensive real estate analysis, the executive team has decided to relocate to a new 250,000 square foot, Class A office building in Hudson Yards. The new space will feature:
- Modern open office design with 40% more collaborative spaces
- State-of-the-art trading floor with redundant systems
- Advanced cybersecurity and physical security infrastructure
- LEED Platinum environmental certification
- Wellness amenities (fitness center, meditation rooms, outdoor terraces)
- Technology-enabled meeting rooms and collaboration spaces

The relocation must be executed with zero business disruption, maintaining 100% operational capability throughout the move. This is particularly critical as the headquarters includes a 24/7 trading desk managing $15 billion in daily transactions, a data center processing 50,000 transactions per second, and customer service operations supporting 500,000 active clients.

## Business Objectives

1. **Business Continuity:** Achieve zero downtime for critical trading and customer service operations
2. **Cost Optimization:** Reduce annual real estate costs by 15% ($4.5M savings annually)
3. **Employee Satisfaction:** Increase employee satisfaction scores from 72% to 85% within 6 months post-move
4. **Talent Attraction:** Support recruitment goals with modern, attractive workspace
5. **Technology Modernization:** Upgrade technology infrastructure to support next 10 years of growth
6. **Sustainability:** Achieve LEED Platinum certification and reduce carbon footprint by 30%
7. **Space Efficiency:** Increase usable space per employee from 150 SF to 208 SF (including collaborative areas)
8. **Brand Enhancement:** Create workspace that reflects company values and strengthens brand identity
9. **Operational Efficiency:** Improve workflow efficiency by 20% through optimized floor layouts
10. **Collaboration:** Increase cross-functional collaboration by 35% through intentional space design

## Key Challenges and Issues

### Challenge 1: Trading Desk 24/7 Operations
**Description:** The trading desk operates 24/7/365 and cannot experience any downtime. The desk manages $15 billion in daily transactions and any interruption could result in millions in losses and regulatory violations.

**Impact:** Critical - Business continuity and regulatory compliance  
**Current Status:** Dual-site operation plan in development  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Maintain parallel operations during transition
- Set up temporary trading floor in new location 2 weeks before move
- Phased migration with redundant systems
- Weekend move for non-trading operations
- 24/7 technical support during transition
- Comprehensive testing 4 weeks before cutover

### Challenge 2: Data Center Migration Complexity
**Description:** Moving a data center processing 50,000 transactions per second with zero data loss and minimal downtime is highly complex and risky.

**Impact:** Critical - Business operations and data integrity  
**Current Status:** Data center strategy being finalized  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Build new data center first, fully operational before migration
- Implement real-time data replication
- Test failover procedures 6 times before move
- Phased application migration over 3-month period
- Maintain old data center operational for 90 days post-move as backup
- Engage specialized data center migration consultant

### Challenge 3: Employee Resistance to Change
**Description:** Many employees have worked in the current location for 10+ years. Open office design reduces private offices from 200 to 80, causing anxiety and resistance among senior staff.

**Impact:** High - Employee morale, retention, and productivity  
**Current Status:** Change management program being developed  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Comprehensive change management and communication program
- Employee involvement in design process (focus groups, surveys)
- Clear office allocation criteria with executive buy-in
- Emphasize benefits: better amenities, technology, collaboration spaces
- Phased orientation and training
- Address concerns proactively through town halls and Q&A sessions

### Challenge 4: Complex Coordination of 50+ Vendors
**Description:** Project involves coordination of 50+ vendors (construction, technology, furniture, movers, etc.) with interdependent schedules and deliverables.

**Impact:** High - Timeline coordination and quality control  
**Current Status:** Vendor management framework being established  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Dedicated vendor management role
- Master project schedule with all vendor dependencies
- Weekly vendor coordination meetings
- Clear contract terms with penalties for delays
- Milestone-based payment structure
- Regular quality inspections and checkpoints

### Challenge 5: Regulatory and Compliance Requirements
**Description:** As a financial services firm, must maintain compliance with SEC, FINRA, and other regulations during move. Records retention, data privacy, and operational continuity are heavily regulated.

**Impact:** Critical - Regulatory compliance and legal risk  
**Current Status:** Compliance requirements being documented  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Compliance officer embedded in project team
- Regulatory requirements checklist for every phase
- External audit of move plan by compliance consultant
- Documented business continuity plan approved by board
- Regular updates to regulatory bodies
- Chain of custody procedures for all records and data

### Challenge 6: Budget Pressure and Cost Overruns
**Description:** Initial budget of $44.5M exceeds approved $42.5M budget. Need to find $2M in savings without compromising quality or critical requirements.

**Impact:** High - Project scope and quality  
**Current Status:** Value engineering in progress  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Value engineering workshops with all stakeholders
- Reduce high-end finishes in non-client-facing areas ($500K)
- Negotiate better pricing through competitive bidding ($750K)
- Reduce furniture upgrades for 300 employees ($400K)
- Defer some amenity buildouts to Phase 2 ($350K)
- Strict change control to prevent scope creep

### Challenge 7: Timeline Pressure Due to Lease Expiration
**Description:** Current lease expires September 30, 2024. Must complete move by end of June to allow 3 months for old space decommissioning. Any construction delays put move date at risk.

**Impact:** Critical - Potential lease penalties or extended dual occupancy costs  
**Current Status:** Aggressive but achievable timeline established  
**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Fast-track permitting process
- Parallel construction paths where possible
- Pre-order long-lead items (furniture, technology)
- Weekly construction progress reviews
- Built-in 2-week buffer before must-move deadline
- Negotiated lease extension option (at premium cost) as backup

## Questions and Exercises for Students

### Exercise 1: Business Continuity Planning
**Scenario:** You must develop a detailed business continuity plan ensuring the trading desk experiences zero downtime during the move. The desk operates 24/7/365 and processes $15 billion daily.

**Tasks:**
1. Design a dual-site operation strategy for the transition period
2. Create a detailed cutover plan with timelines and responsibilities
3. Identify all potential failure points and mitigation strategies
4. Develop testing procedures to validate the continuity plan
5. Create a rollback plan if the cutover fails
6. Calculate the cost of your business continuity approach

### Exercise 2: Budget Optimization Exercise
**Scenario:** You need to reduce the budget from $44.5M to $42.5M (save $2M) without compromising critical requirements or quality standards.

**Tasks:**
1. Analyze each budget category and identify reduction opportunities
2. Create a value engineering matrix prioritizing potential savings
3. Assess the impact and risks of each cost reduction
4. Recommend specific cuts totaling $2M with justification
5. Identify which items are non-negotiable and why
6. Present your recommendations to stakeholders (role-play)

### Exercise 3: Stakeholder Management Challenge
**Scenario:** Senior executives are resisting the move to open office design. The CFO wants private offices maintained (200 vs. planned 80). This would add $2M to the budget and reduce collaborative space by 40%.

**Tasks:**
1. Analyze stakeholder positions and concerns
2. Develop a stakeholder engagement strategy
3. Create data-driven arguments for open office benefits
4. Design a compromise solution if needed
5. Prepare a presentation to address executive concerns
6. Draft a change management plan to ease the transition

### Exercise 4: Critical Path Analysis
**Tasks:**
1. Identify the critical path in the 18-month project schedule
2. Calculate float/slack for non-critical activities
3. Determine which activities can be fast-tracked or crashed
4. Analyze the impact of construction delays on move date
5. Develop a recovery plan if construction is delayed by 6 weeks
6. Create a "what-if" scenario analysis for various delays

### Exercise 5: Risk Management Simulation
**Scenario:** Multiple risks have materialized simultaneously:
- Construction is 4 weeks behind schedule (it's now March 15)
- A key technology vendor has gone bankrupt
- 50 senior employees have signed a petition against open office design
- Budget is tracking 12% over due to unforeseen issues

**Tasks:**
1. Assess the severity and interconnection of these risks
2. Prioritize which risks to address first and why
3. Develop recovery plans for each risk
4. Estimate the total impact on timeline and budget
5. Determine if the June 30 move date is still achievable
6. Prepare an executive briefing on the situation and recommendations

### Exercise 6: Vendor Coordination Exercise
**Scenario:** You have 50+ vendors working on the project. Create a vendor management framework.

**Tasks:**
1. Develop a vendor coordination schedule showing all dependencies
2. Create a vendor performance scorecard and KPIs
3. Design an escalation process for vendor issues
4. Draft contract terms including penalties for delays
5. Create a vendor communication protocol
6. Develop contingency plans for vendor failures

### Exercise 7: Move Weekend Planning
**Scenario:** Plan the detailed logistics for the June 28-30 move weekend.

**Tasks:**
1. Create a hour-by-hour schedule for the 3-day move
2. Assign roles and responsibilities to all team members
3. Design the floor-by-floor move sequence
4. Plan IT disconnect/reconnect procedures
5. Develop a command center structure for move coordination
6. Create contingency plans for common move day issues
7. Design a communication protocol for the move weekend

### Exercise 8: Technology Migration Planning
**Scenario:** Develop a detailed plan to migrate the data center with zero data loss and minimal downtime.

**Tasks:**
1. Design the data center migration strategy (phased vs. big bang)
2. Create a detailed application migration sequence
3. Develop testing procedures for each migration phase
4. Calculate the optimal cutover window
5. Design the data replication and synchronization approach
6. Create rollback procedures for each application
7. Estimate the risk of data loss and how to prevent it

### Exercise 9: Change Management Strategy
**Scenario:** Develop a comprehensive change management program for the 1,200 employees.

**Tasks:**
1. Conduct a change impact assessment by employee group
2. Design a multi-channel communication strategy
3. Create training programs for new systems and space
4. Develop employee engagement tactics to build excitement
5. Design a change champion network structure
6. Create metrics to measure change adoption and resistance
7. Develop interventions for resistant employee groups

### Exercise 10: Post-Move Performance Analysis
**Scenario:** It's 90 days post-move (September 30). Analyze results and recommend improvements.

**Results:**
- Move completed on June 30 (on time) ✓
- Final budget: $43.2M (vs. $42.5M target) ✗
- Zero trading downtime achieved ✓
- Employee satisfaction: 78% (vs. 85% target) ✗
- 15 punch list items still open ✗
- Productivity returned to baseline in 3 weeks (vs. 2-week target) ✗
- Cost savings: $4.2M annual (vs. $4.5M target) ✗

**Tasks:**
1. Analyze what went well and what didn't
2. Identify root causes for missed targets
3. Develop action plans to close gaps (employee satisfaction, punch list)
4. Calculate actual ROI including overrun
5. Extract lessons learned for future projects
6. Create recommendations for ongoing optimization

### Exercise 11: Regulatory Compliance Exercise
**Scenario:** As a financial services firm, ensure full regulatory compliance during the move.

**Tasks:**
1. Identify all applicable regulations (SEC, FINRA, SOX, GDPR, etc.)
2. Create a compliance checklist for each project phase
3. Develop data handling procedures that maintain compliance
4. Design audit trails for records and data migration
5. Create a regulatory communication plan
6. Prepare compliance documentation for board review

### Exercise 12: Sustainability and LEED Certification
**Scenario:** Achieve LEED Platinum certification while managing costs.

**Tasks:**
1. Identify LEED requirements and credit opportunities
2. Conduct cost-benefit analysis of various LEED strategies
3. Recommend which LEED credits to pursue
4. Develop a documentation plan for LEED certification
5. Calculate the additional cost for LEED Platinum vs. Gold
6. Estimate the ROI of sustainability investments
