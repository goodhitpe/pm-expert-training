# Recommended Books for Project Managers

A comprehensive, curated list of essential reading for project managers at all levels.

## Essential PM Books (Fundamentals)

### 1. A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK Guide)
- **Author:** Project Management Institute (PMI)
- **Year:** 7th Edition (2021)
- **Korean Title:** PMBOK 가이드
- **Summary:** The definitive guide to project management standards and best practices. Covers the full project lifecycle, knowledge areas, and process groups. The 7th edition emphasizes principles, domains, and tailoring over strict processes.
- **Who Should Read:** All PMs, especially those preparing for PMP certification. Essential reference material.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Standard terminology and processes for project management
  - 12 project management principles
  - 8 project performance domains
  - How to tailor approaches to different project contexts

### 2. The Fast Forward MBA in Project Management
- **Author:** Eric Verzuh
- **Year:** 5th Edition (2015)
- **Korean Title:** 프로젝트 관리 Fast Forward MBA
- **Summary:** A practical, accessible introduction to project management without the complexity. Covers essential PM concepts, tools, and techniques in a clear, business-focused manner.
- **Who Should Read:** New project managers, business professionals transitioning into PM roles.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Quick overview of PM fundamentals
  - Practical tools and templates
  - Real-world application examples
  - Integration of traditional and agile approaches

### 3. Project Management: The Managerial Process
- **Author:** Erik W. Larson and Clifford F. Gray
- **Year:** 8th Edition (2020)
- **Korean Title:** 프로젝트 경영론
- **Summary:** Comprehensive textbook covering both technical and behavioral aspects of project management. Excellent for academic understanding with practical applications.
- **Who Should Read:** Students, new PMs, those seeking deep theoretical understanding.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Strategic management and project selection
  - Project organization and leadership
  - Risk management frameworks
  - International and multicultural project management

### 4. Making Things Happen: Mastering Project Management
- **Author:** Scott Berkun
- **Year:** 2008
- **Korean Title:** 프로젝트가 서쪽으로 간 까닭은
- **Summary:** Practical wisdom from Microsoft veteran covering the human side of project management. Focuses on decision-making, leadership, and real-world problem-solving.
- **Who Should Read:** Experienced PMs looking for practical insights, tech industry PMs.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - How to make difficult decisions
  - Managing stakeholder relationships
  - Dealing with project chaos
  - Learning from failure

## Agile & Scrum Books

### 5. Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time
- **Author:** Jeff Sutherland
- **Year:** 2014
- **Korean Title:** 스크럼: 팀의 생산성을 두 배로 높이는 비결
- **Summary:** Written by the co-creator of Scrum, this book explains the framework's origins, principles, and practical implementation. Includes real-world success stories from various industries.
- **Who Should Read:** Anyone new to Scrum, leaders considering agile transformation.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Origins and principles of Scrum
  - How to implement Scrum effectively
  - Common pitfalls and how to avoid them
  - Scrum beyond software development

### 6. User Stories Applied: For Agile Software Development
- **Author:** Mike Cohn
- **Year:** 2004
- **Korean Title:** 사용자 스토리
- **Summary:** Definitive guide to writing effective user stories. Covers the entire lifecycle from gathering requirements to acceptance testing.
- **Who Should Read:** Scrum Masters, Product Owners, Agile PMs.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - How to write effective user stories
  - INVEST criteria for good stories
  - Estimation techniques
  - Managing the product backlog

### 7. The Lean Startup
- **Author:** Eric Ries
- **Year:** 2011
- **Korean Title:** 린 스타트업
- **Summary:** Revolutionary approach to creating and managing startups through validated learning, experimentation, and iterative product releases. Applies lean principles to product development.
- **Who Should Read:** Product managers, startup founders, innovation teams.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop
  - Minimum Viable Product (MVP) concept
  - Validated learning over assumptions
  - Pivot or persevere decisions

### 8. Agile Estimating and Planning
- **Author:** Mike Cohn
- **Year:** 2005
- **Korean Title:** 애자일 추정과 계획
- **Summary:** Comprehensive guide to estimation and planning in agile projects. Covers story points, planning poker, release planning, and velocity.
- **Who Should Read:** Scrum Masters, Agile coaches, team leads.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Story point estimation techniques
  - Planning poker method
  - Release and iteration planning
  - Managing velocity and forecasting

### 9. The Phoenix Project
- **Author:** Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford
- **Year:** 2013
- **Korean Title:** 피닉스 프로젝트
- **Summary:** A novel about IT DevOps and project management. Illustrates the Theory of Constraints and DevOps principles through an engaging story.
- **Who Should Read:** IT project managers, DevOps practitioners, technical leaders.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Three Ways of DevOps
  - Managing work in progress
  - Theory of Constraints application
  - Cultural transformation in IT

## Leadership & Soft Skills Books

### 10. Crucial Conversations: Tools for Talking When Stakes Are High
- **Author:** Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan, Al Switzler
- **Year:** 2nd Edition (2011)
- **Korean Title:** 크루셜 대화
- **Summary:** Essential guide to handling difficult conversations. Provides frameworks for dialogue when stakes are high, opinions vary, and emotions run strong.
- **Who Should Read:** All PMs, team leaders, anyone managing stakeholder relationships.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - How to stay in dialogue during conflict
  - STATE framework for crucial conversations
  - Creating safety in high-stakes discussions
  - Moving from talk to action

### 11. The Five Dysfunctions of a Team
- **Author:** Patrick Lencioni
- **Year:** 2002
- **Korean Title:** 팀의 5가지 법칙
- **Summary:** A leadership fable about teamwork. Identifies five dysfunctions that hold teams back and provides actionable solutions.
- **Who Should Read:** Team leaders, project managers, organizational leaders.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Building trust within teams
  - Mastering productive conflict
  - Achieving commitment
  - Embracing accountability
  - Focus on collective results

### 12. Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us
- **Author:** Daniel H. Pink
- **Year:** 2009
- **Korean Title:** 드라이브
- **Summary:** Explores intrinsic motivation and what truly drives people. Challenges traditional reward-punishment models and presents autonomy, mastery, and purpose as key motivators.
- **Who Should Read:** All managers, leaders, HR professionals.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Autonomy, Mastery, and Purpose (AMP)
  - Limitations of extrinsic motivation
  - Creating motivating environments
  - Type I vs Type X behavior

### 13. Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion
- **Author:** Robert B. Cialdini
- **Year:** Revised Edition (2006)
- **Korean Title:** 설득의 심리학
- **Summary:** Classic book on the psychology of influence. Identifies six universal principles of persuasion backed by scientific research.
- **Who Should Read:** All PMs, sales professionals, negotiators.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Six principles of influence: reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity
  - Ethical persuasion techniques
  - Defending against manipulation
  - Building stakeholder buy-in

### 14. Emotional Intelligence 2.0
- **Author:** Travis Bradberry and Jean Greaves
- **Year:** 2009
- **Korean Title:** 감성지능 2.0
- **Summary:** Practical guide to developing emotional intelligence. Includes self-assessment and 66 proven strategies to improve EQ.
- **Who Should Read:** All leaders and managers.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Four core EQ skills: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management
  - Practical strategies to improve each skill
  - Impact of EQ on performance
  - Assessment and measurement

### 15. Radical Candor: Be a Kick-Ass Boss Without Losing Your Humanity
- **Author:** Kim Scott
- **Year:** 2017
- **Korean Title:** 급진적 솔직함
- **Summary:** Framework for giving feedback that's both caring and direct. Balances challenging directly with caring personally.
- **Who Should Read:** Managers, team leads, mentors.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Radical Candor framework
  - Care personally + Challenge directly
  - Avoiding ruinous empathy and manipulative insincerity
  - Building trust through feedback

## Risk Management & Decision Making

### 16. The Checklist Manifesto
- **Author:** Atul Gawande
- **Year:** 2009
- **Korean Title:** 체크! 체크리스트
- **Summary:** Explores how simple checklists can prevent errors and improve outcomes in complex environments, from surgery to aviation to construction.
- **Who Should Read:** All PMs, quality managers, process improvement professionals.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Power of simple checklists
  - READ-DO vs DO-CONFIRM checklists
  - Designing effective checklists
  - Overcoming resistance to checklists

### 17. Thinking, Fast and Slow
- **Author:** Daniel Kahneman
- **Year:** 2011
- **Korean Title:** 생각에 관한 생각
- **Summary:** Nobel Prize winner's exploration of two systems of thinking: fast, intuitive thinking and slow, deliberate thinking. Reveals cognitive biases that affect decision-making.
- **Who Should Read:** All decision-makers, strategists, analysts.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - System 1 (fast) vs System 2 (slow) thinking
  - Common cognitive biases
  - Making better decisions under uncertainty
  - The planning fallacy

### 18. The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable
- **Author:** Nassim Nicholas Taleb
- **Year:** 2007
- **Korean Title:** 블랙 스완
- **Summary:** Explores the extreme impact of rare, unpredictable events and our tendency to find simplistic explanations for them after the fact.
- **Who Should Read:** Risk managers, strategists, senior PMs.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Understanding black swan events
  - Limitations of forecasting and models
  - Building robustness and antifragility
  - Preparing for the unpredictable

## Product Management & Strategy

### 19. Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love
- **Author:** Marty Cagan
- **Year:** 2nd Edition (2017)
- **Korean Title:** 인스파이어드
- **Summary:** Essential reading for product managers. Covers product discovery, validation, and delivery at leading tech companies.
- **Who Should Read:** Product managers, product owners, startup founders.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Product discovery techniques
  - Building strong product teams
  - Continuous product validation
  - Product leadership principles

### 20. Crossing the Chasm
- **Author:** Geoffrey A. Moore
- **Year:** 3rd Edition (2014)
- **Korean Title:** 캐즘 마케팅
- **Summary:** Marketing strategy for technology products. Explains the gap between early adopters and mainstream market and how to cross it.
- **Who Should Read:** Product managers, marketers, startup founders.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Technology adoption lifecycle
  - The chasm between early adopters and early majority
  - Strategies for crossing the chasm
  - Target market selection

## Industry-Specific PM Books

### 21. Software Project Management (IT/Software)
- **Author:** Bob Hughes, Mike Cotterell, and Rajib Mall
- **Year:** 6th Edition (2016)
- **Summary:** Focused on software project management challenges, including estimation, scheduling, risk management, and team management specific to software development.
- **Who Should Read:** IT project managers, software development leads.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Software estimation techniques (COCOMO, function points)
  - Managing distributed teams
  - Software-specific risk factors
  - Configuration management

### 22. Construction Project Management (Construction)
- **Author:** Alison Dykstra
- **Year:** 2019
- **Summary:** Comprehensive guide to managing construction projects from initiation through closeout, including contracts, procurement, and safety.
- **Who Should Read:** Construction project managers, civil engineers.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Construction project lifecycle
  - Contract types and procurement
  - Safety management
  - Quality control in construction

### 23. The AMA Handbook of Project Management (Multi-Industry)
- **Author:** Paul C. Dinsmore and Jeannette Cabanis-Brewin
- **Year:** 4th Edition (2014)
- **Summary:** Comprehensive handbook covering PM best practices across industries. Includes contributions from leading practitioners.
- **Who Should Read:** All PMs seeking comprehensive reference.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Best practices across industries
  - Portfolio and program management
  - PM maturity models
  - Global project management

## Advanced & Specialized Topics

### 27. Program Management Professional (PgMP) Exam Preparation
- **Author:** Ginger Levin
- **Year:** 2019
- **Summary:** Comprehensive guide to program management and PgMP certification. Covers managing multiple related projects and achieving strategic objectives.
- **Who Should Read:** Senior PMs, program managers, PgMP candidates.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Program vs project management
  - Program governance
  - Benefits realization
  - Stakeholder engagement at program level

### 28. Waltzing with Bears: Managing Risk on Software Projects
- **Author:** Tom DeMarco and Timothy Lister
- **Year:** 2003
- **Summary:** Focused approach to risk management in software projects. Argues that risk management is essential, not optional.
- **Who Should Read:** Software PMs, risk managers, IT leaders.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Core risks in software projects
  - Quantifying uncertainty
  - Risk-driven scheduling
  - Creating risk management culture

### 29. The Mythical Man-Month
- **Author:** Frederick P. Brooks Jr.
- **Year:** Anniversary Edition (1995)
- **Korean Title:** 맨먼스 미신
- **Summary:** Classic book on software engineering and project management. Famous for Brooks's Law: "Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later."
- **Who Should Read:** Software PMs, technical leads, software engineers.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Brooks's Law and its implications
  - The second-system effect
  - Communication overhead in large teams
  - Conceptual integrity in design

### 30. Leading Change
- **Author:** John P. Kotter
- **Year:** 2012
- **Korean Title:** 기업이 원하는 변화
- **Summary:** Eight-step process for leading organizational change. Essential for PMs involved in transformation projects.
- **Who Should Read:** Change managers, organizational leaders, senior PMs.
- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Eight-step change process
  - Creating urgency for change
  - Building guiding coalitions
  - Anchoring change in culture
